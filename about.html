Host & Creator
My Story

From Boardrooms
to Dharmshalas

I didn't set out to start a podcast. I set out to find the conversations that nobody seemed to be having — the ones that happen at 2am, over chai, between two people who've stopped pretending they have it all figured out.

Namah Talks started as a small experiment: what if I just recorded the conversations I was already having? What if I brought a microphone to the moments that actually matter? What followed was 200+ episodes, guests from 20+ countries, and a community of listeners who care as deeply about living intentionally as I do.

I believe the most important questions we can ask are the ones without easy answers. That's what we explore here — the messy, beautiful, uncertain reality of being human in a world that's changing faster than any of us can keep up with.

From founders to philosophers, monks to money-makers, artists to activists — every guest comes with a story that shifts how you see the world. That's the only criterion for an invitation.
